Planify helps students and teams quickly organize tasks, track progress, and review monthly results in a clean, accessible interface

What it is

An online planner for creating and saving daily, weekly, and monthly plans with clear progress tracking

The problem

Many students lose track of tasks, miss deadlines, and cannot easily review their progress

Why it matters

A good plan saves time, improves focus, and helps students reach study goals more reliably

What makes it special

Simplicity, monthly summaries, and account support make Planify a practical tool for everyday planning.

Project overview

Planify is a web app for creating plans where users can build a daily, weekly, or monthly schedule, save it to a personal account, and review results at the end of each period

  • What it is: a planner for tasks and study routines
  • The problem it solves: lack of structure and control over task completion
  • Why this matters: it helps users stay organized and improve productivity
  • What makes it unique: quick setup, easy registration, and clear monthly summaries

Features

Step 1. Create a plan

Choose the period, list tasks, and set priorities to build a structured schedule

Step 2. Register account

Save plans to a personal profile so you can access them from any device

Step 3. Review results

Monthly summaries show completed tasks and areas that need more attention

Step 4. Clear design

A white and gold palette keeps the interface calm, modern, and easy to use

Sponsor Interview

Aibar: Hi Aliaskar, thank you for being the sponsor for Planify. To start, could you tell us about your background and your interest in supporting a project focused on goal setting and scheduling?
Aliaskar: I’ve spent a lot of time managing different tasks, and I’ve seen how easy it is for people to lose track of long term goals when they get overwhelmed by daily chores. I’m interested in supporting student projects that solve real-world productivity gaps using clean, functional tech.
Aibar: We noticed that many people make daily lists but forget their yearly ambitions. Why do you think this disconnect is a problem worth solving?
Aliaskar: It’s a major issue because without a big picture, daily tasks feel like a treadmill. People burn out because they don't see progress toward their actual life goals. It’s a community problem; if students or professionals can't plan, they can't reach their full potential.
Aibar: What would make Planify worth your support? What impact should it have on a user to make you proud of the project?
Aliaskar: I’d be proud if a user told me they finally finished a yearly goal, like learning a language or a new skill, because the app broke it down into daily steps. It needs to promote intentional living rather than just busy work
Aibar: We are targeting students and young professionals. Who do you think would benefit most from this daily to yearly system?
Aliaskar: Definitely students in intense programs or early career professionals. They need to gain the skill of prioritization. The app shouldn't just list tasks; it should help them decide what is actually important for their future.
Aibar: In an app like Planify, what are the must have features for you, and what should we avoid?
Aliaskar: A must have is a visual link between a daily task and a larger goal. If I’m doing a workout today, I want to see how it contributes to my yearly health goal. Avoid feature creep, don't add too many buttons or social media sharing. Keep it focused and minimalistic.
Aibar: Since this is a student project, do you have any concerns about our scope or the technical difficulty of building this?
Aliaskar: My main concern is the data structure. Linking yearly, monthly, and daily data can get messy. I suggest you focus on a very solid Daily and Weekly core first. Ensure the progress journal reflects how you handle these technical hurdles.
Aibar: Finally, what is the one thing that would convince you to fully support Planify, and what advice would you give us before we start coding?
\Aliaskar: I’ll support it if the UI is clean and it actually works without bugs. My advice: Start small. Build a perfect daily planner first, then expand to the yearly view. If the foundation is weak, the rest won't matter.
